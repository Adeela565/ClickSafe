That VPN & MFA update email was a phishing simulation.

Attackers often pretend to be IT asking you to “re-enroll MFA” or “update VPN settings.” This simulation showed how those requests can be abused to steal your credentials.

Red flags in this email

  • Unexpected IT change – no prior notice from official IT channels.
  • Pressure to re-enroll MFA or update VPN immediately or risk losing access.
  • Link did not point to your real corporate VPN/MFA portal when you hovered over it.
  • Sender address not matching internal IT or security addresses your company uses.
  • Instructions to bypass normal login flows (e.g., “turn off MFA first, then…”) – a big red flag.

How to verify IT notices

Check the IT or Security channel on Teams / intranet
Look for announcements on the official IT status page
Use your usual VPN/MFA app or bookmark instead of the email link
Contact the Service Desk if anything feels off

Before acting on any IT request…

  • Ask yourself if this matches other communications you’ve seen (town halls, IT notices, etc.).
  • Be wary of emails asking you to log in with personal accounts (e.g., Gmail, personal Microsoft ID).
  • Never approve MFA prompts you did not initiate yourself.

Do’s and Don’ts

Do:

  • Use MFA wherever possible, especially for VPN and email.
  • Lock your device if you see unexpected MFA prompts and call IT immediately.
  • Keep your VPN and MFA apps updated via official app stores.

Don’t:

  • Enter your work password on portals you reached from an unexpected email.
  • Approve MFA requests that pop up when you’re not logging in.
  • Disable security controls just because an email told you to.

Policy reminder

For any IT-related change:
  • Expect communication from official IT channels, not just email.
  • IT will never ask for your password over email or chat.
  • Report suspicious IT notices to the Service Desk or Security team.
